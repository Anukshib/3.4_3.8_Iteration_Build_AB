ANCIENT RUINS

DESTINATION 1

Anuradhapura: One of Sri Lankas ancient capitals, Anuradhapura is renowned for its well-preserved ruins. Key attractions include the sacred Sri Maha Bodhi tree (a sapling from the original tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment), the towering Ruwanwelisaya stupa, and the massive Jetavanaramaya stupa. The city was a center of Theravada Buddhism and features numerous ancient temples, monasteries, and water reservoirs.

DESTINATION 2

Polonnaruwa: This ancient city served as the capital of Sri Lanka after the fall of Anuradhapura. Highlights include the Gal Vihara complex, which contains impressive rock-cut Buddha statues, and the Parakrama Samudra, a massive irrigation reservoir. The ruins also include the Royal Palace, the Council Chamber, and several intricately designed temples and statues.

DESTINATION 3

Dambulla Cave Temple: Located in central Sri Lanka, the Dambulla Cave Temple complex consists of five caves adorned with Buddhist murals and statues. The caves contain impressive rock carvings and more than 150 Buddha statues, making it an important religious and historical site.
